The Underlying View Color Preference Panel

    This preference panel gives control of the ScrollingWindow class's view colors (the View color, the High color and the Low color). Changing the High color changes the underlying color for all the application's windows. The View color and High color should remain identical, otherwise when the window redraws a brief flash of the View color will show, which can be annoying on slower systems, especially if the colors clash! This is because the window is cleared to the view color before a re-draw. The Low color has no obvious affect. All three of these colors may affect how the other gadgets look. Some BeOS Interface Kit classes take their color information from their underlying view and not themselves. I consider this a bug, and will lobby to have it changed!
